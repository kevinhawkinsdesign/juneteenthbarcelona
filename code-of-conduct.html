Community

Code of
Conduct

Last updated: April 2026

Juneteenth Barcelona is a celebration of Black liberation, culture, and community. Everyone who attends — regardless of background — is a guest in a space built on joy, dignity, and respect. We expect all attendees, volunteers, performers, vendors, and partners to honour that.

Our Commitment

We are committed to providing a safe, welcoming, and harassment-free experience for everyone. Juneteenth Barcelona does not tolerate discrimination, harassment, or intimidation in any form — whether based on race, ethnicity, nationality, gender, gender identity or expression, sexual orientation, disability, age, religion, or any other characteristic.

Expected Behaviour

  • Treat every person with respect and kindness
  • Be mindful of the cultural significance of the space you are in
  • Use inclusive language and be open to correction if you cause unintended harm
  • Respect personal boundaries — ask before photographing or touching others
  • Follow the instructions of event staff, security, and venue personnel
  • Leave the space as you found it and take care of shared resources

Unacceptable Behaviour

The following will not be tolerated at any Juneteenth Barcelona event or gathering:

  • Harassment, intimidation, or threatening behaviour of any kind
  • Racist, sexist, homophobic, transphobic, ableist, or otherwise discriminatory language or actions
  • Unwanted physical contact or sexual attention
  • Deliberate misgendering or use of chosen names without consent
  • Recording or photographing people without their consent
  • Possession or use of illegal substances on venue premises
  • Disruptive behaviour that degrades the experience for others
  • Retaliation against anyone who reports a concern

A Note on Cultural Space

Juneteenth is a holiday rooted in African-American history and the broader African diaspora. Our events are designed as a space of belonging for Black and diaspora communities. We welcome all guests to attend in a spirit of solidarity, curiosity, and respect — not as observers, but as participants who understand the history and honour its significance.

Reporting

If you experience or witness behaviour that violates this Code of Conduct, please speak to any event staff member wearing a volunteer lanyard, or contact us via the contact page. All reports will be taken seriously, handled discreetly, and acted on promptly.

Consequences

Anyone asked to stop unacceptable behaviour is expected to comply immediately. The Organisers reserve the right to remove any person from the event without refund for violating this Code of Conduct. In serious cases, incidents may be reported to local authorities.

Scope

This Code of Conduct applies to all Juneteenth Barcelona events — including the main June 20 event, monthly meetups, online spaces, and any other gathering organised under the Juneteenth Barcelona name.

Questions

If you have questions about this Code of Conduct, please reach out via the contact page.
